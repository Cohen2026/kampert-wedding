Our story

A little girl from Haiti,
a little boy from the Netherlands.

Our journey began unexpectedly in a season where both of us were focused on becoming better individually — growing in purpose, clarity, and direction. Neither of us was looking for anything forced or rushed, just something real, peaceful, and aligned with who we were becoming.

What started as a simple conversation quickly became something we both recognized as different from our norm. There was an ease in how we spoke, a depth that came naturally, and a peace that continued to confirm itself with time — everything unfolded with intention and alignment.

From the beginning, we chose to get to know each other intentionally. Our conversations moved beyond surface level into life, family, values, purpose, and vision for the future. We both knew what we wanted and desired to do things differently this time — right, with God as the foundation. We asked real questions and had uncomfortable conversations that needed to be had, especially as things began moving at a surprising pace, yet it still felt like we had known each other for much longer than we actually had.

We also come from two very different worlds. Cleo is Haitian, from Haiti, raised in Orlando, Florida, and Paul is Dutch, from the Netherlands, living in Tampa, Florida. Different cultures, different backgrounds, and different paths — or so it seemed — but somehow, our lives crossed in Florida at the right time in a way we could not have planned ourselves. Who would have thought that a little girl from Haiti and a little boy from the Netherlands would one day cross paths like this?… well, not us!

As we continued talking and spending time together, what stood out most was consistency, peace, and intentionality. We weren't approaching this casually. We were both in a season of growth, and we allowed our connection to develop naturally.

With time, what we built became something steady. We learned how to communicate, how to understand one another, and how to appreciate not just our similarities, but also our differences. Through it all, we discovered a relationship grounded in partnership — where we support each other, challenge each other to grow, and genuinely enjoy doing life together.

What has made this journey so meaningful is that it has never felt forced. We have continually experienced a peace that guided us forward, and the quiet confirmation of God's presence revealing something deeper than either of us expected in the beginning.

Today, we are grateful for the way our paths crossed and for everything that has led us to this moment. By God's grace, we look forward to stepping into this next chapter as Mr. & Mrs. Kampert.

Good questions

In case you
were wondering.

  • Open House is a beautifully restored indoor venue, with an open-air courtyard for cocktails. You're covered either way — cool, dry, and ready for July in Florida.

  • Warm, soft evenings — usually high seventies once the sun is low, with the chance of a brief afternoon shower. We'll have umbrellas, hand fans and cold towels on standby.

  • We adore yours. The reception is best enjoyed as an adults-only evening, with named exceptions on the invitation. Local sitter recommendations available on request.

  • Your presence is everything. If you'd still like to celebrate with a gift, we're saving for a long, slow honeymoon — there's a small fund linked on our welcome card.

  • The on-site lot is complimentary, with additional street parking along Lee Road. Rideshare is welcomed and arguably wiser if you'd like a second glass of champagne.

  • For anything at all, write to hello@thekamperts.com — replies usually arrive within a day.
